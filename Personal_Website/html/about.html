Troy Kelley is a Connecticut native, living in Stamford, CT his whole life. There he danced at Connecticut Ballet as a trainee with Oksana Maslova, Brett Raphael, and Marc Spielberger. He has also studied at Stamford Ballet Center, where he was taught by Heather Hawk, and Eric Otto. Troy attends NYU studying Computer Science and Dance, where he is now a senior. During this time in the Dance program, Troy has worked with members of the Trisha Brown, David Dorfman, and Arnie Zane/Bill T. Jones companies to learn and perform their repertoire at the Tisch Dance Summer Dance Festival 2018. He has studied in Austria at the Salzburg Experimental Academy of Dance (SEAD), learning the styles of and creating works under the supervision of Eleonore Valere Lachky, and Vittoria De Ferrari Sapetto.

Troy has been part of the first university-level performance of London-based choreographer Wayne McGregor, dancing an excerpt of F.A.R. in October 2019. Troy has also had the opportunity to work with Ronald K. Brown and Arcell Cabuag from Evidence Dance Company performing Upside Down, as well as with Lar Lubovitch in Marimba. In Spring 2020, Troy worked with Wendy Perron in the first ever reconstruction of Trisha Brown’s Splang. Troy’s heart belongs onstage, but he is also interested in creating his own works in the studio and sharing his passion for dance with others. At The Darien Arts Center in Darien CT, Troy taught as an instructor for two years, teaching students tap, ballet, jazz, and contemporary. There he also created numerous works for the performance company, working with Bonnie Gombos, Neeta Vencak, and Emily Frangipane. Troy has choreographed several pieces during his time at NYU, most recently choreographing SUUBNLIMNATLWYNVECNSABMGOUSBCNSCS (Liminal) for the Second Avenue Dance Company. This piece was re-imagined for screen in Spring 2020 for a virtual performance with orginal music by Garrison Ho and self-made video production.

Troy is also an avid computer programmer and web developer. He has a passion for everything technology, from mobile computing to the fundamentals of backend systems. He uses his programming knowledge in tandem with his web development skills to design fully-featured, beautiful software. He is currently focusing on using his multi-faceted knowledge to break the barrier between dance and technology. He wishes to realize new ways to bring advanced technolgies to the dance space as well bring performative experiences to our devices.

Troy is currently the Technical Lead for the Strikethrough Score Project by Maxine Flasher-Düzgüneş, which explores the process of erasure in text as an interpretive score for dance. The website Strikethrough-score.org has been featured in the Opening Show of the digital museum The Museum of Wild and Newfangled Art.
